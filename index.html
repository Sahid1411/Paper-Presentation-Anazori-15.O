Rules & Guidelines

Please adhere to the following rules for a fair competition.

1. Presentation Duration

  • Maximum Time Allowed: 10 minutes, including the introduction, content delivery, and conclusion.
  • Exceeding the time limit will result in a gentle reminder from the moderator, and presentations may be cut off if significantly over time.

2. Structure & Flow

  • Introduction (1 minute): Presenters should briefly introduce themselves and the topic.
  • Main Content (7 minutes): Key points must be delivered concisely, focusing on the most relevant and impactful aspects of the subject.
  • Conclusion (1 minute): Summarize the presentation's main ideas and provide a final thought or takeaway.
  • Optional Q&A: If allowed, presenters may use the final minute for a brief interaction with the audience.

3. Visual Aids & Slides

  • Slide Count: Limit the number of slides to 6-8 to avoid overwhelming the audience.
  • Clarity & Simplicity: Slides should be minimalistic, avoiding excessive text. Focus on visuals, graphs, and key points.
  • Font & Size: Use legible fonts and sizes (minimum 24pt for text).
  • Compatibility: Ensure your presentation format (e.g., PowerPoint, PDF) is compatible with the event’s AV system.

4. Technical Setup

  • Check-in Time: Presenters must arrive at least 30 minutes before the session begins for a technical check.
  • Backup: Bring a copy of the presentation on a USB drive and upload it to a cloud platform.
  • Mic & Sound Check: Verify the microphone and AV setup before the presentation begins.

5. Content & Presentation Etiquette

  • The content must align with the assigned topic and should focus on meaningful insights, research, or case studies.
  • Engage the audience through interactive elements, thought-provoking questions, or real-life examples.
  • Presenters should dress appropriately and maintain a professional tone.
  • Maintain eye contact with the audience and use gestures to emphasize points, avoiding reading from slides.

6. Submission Requirements

  • Provide references for all sources used in the research.
  • Submit a hardcopy of the researched paper on the day of the event.

Evaluation Criteria

Presentations will be evaluated based on the following:

  1. Clarity & Organization: How well the presentation is structured and easy to follow.
  2. Content Relevance: The relevance and depth of the content presented.
  3. Time Management: Staying within the 10-minute time limit.
  4. Engagement: The ability to maintain audience interest and interaction.
  5. Use of Visuals: The effectiveness of slides and visual aids in complementing the talk.

** The decision of the judge(s) will be final and binding. **
